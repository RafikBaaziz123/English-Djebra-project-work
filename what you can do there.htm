Houmt Souk Old Town

with its quaint maze of alleyways lined by picturesque whitewashed houses and shops selling colorful ceramics, the Old Town district of Houmt Souk(Djerba's main town) was made for strolling.

A number of well-preserved fondouks (caravanserais) can be seen amid Houmt souK's alleways

These merchant inns combined sleeping quarters, animal stabling, and warehouse storage for the many travelling merchants who traversed North Africa, buying up salt, spices, and textiles to take back to Europe.

Djerba Explore Park

Djerba Explore Park sits on the island's coastal road, 20 Km east of Houmt Souk. The complex combines the Lalla Hadria Museum and the Djerba explore Crocodile Farm.

The purpose-built and contemporary Lalla Hadria Museum is devoted to Islamic cultural heritage and art. It contains an impressive collection of over 1,000 pieces displayed across 15 galleries.

La Ghriba Synagogue

La Ghriba is Djerba's most prominent reminder of the island's vibrant Jewish community, which has now disppeared.

Although not particularly impressive from the outside and not that old either (the synagogue was built in the 1920s), this site has a history that goes backkmuch further.

One locan story says that a holy stone fell to earth here denoting the site's religious impotance.

every year, 33 days after Easter, La Ghriba is the scene of the Maghreb's (north africa's) most important Jewish pilgrimage.
